About Greenfield University

Our story, mission, and vision

🎯 Our Mission

Our mission is to cultivate an intellectually stimulating environment where students are empowered to think critically, act ethically, and contribute meaningfully to society. We foster innovation through interdisciplinary research and experiential learning.

We are committed to providing accessible, high-quality education to students from all backgrounds, ensuring every graduate is equipped with the skills to thrive in a rapidly evolving global economy.

🌟 Our Vision

Greenfield University envisions a future where knowledge is the greatest equalizer. We strive to be Africa's leading research university, recognized globally for academic excellence, social impact, and transformative innovation.

By 2030, we aim to expand our international partnerships to over 500 institutions, increase our research output by 200%, and build the largest STEM facility in East Africa.

Our History

Greenfield University was established in 1985 by a consortium of visionary educators who believed that East Africa needed a world-class institution that could rival global universities while remaining rooted in African values and culture.

Over the decades, Greenfield has grown from a small college of 200 students to a thriving university community of over 25,000 students and 2,000 staff members. Our alumni network spans over 80 countries.

  • 1985 — University founded with 3 faculties
  • 1990 — First postgraduate programs launched
  • 1998 — ISO Accreditation received
  • 2005 — New main campus opened
  • 2015 — Online learning platform launched
  • 2020 — Ranked Top 10 in Africa
  • 2026 — 25,000+ active students
